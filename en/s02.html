France just drew the under-16 line

France has become the first EU country to pass a blanket ban on social media accounts for under-16s. Platforms get six months to verify age — or face fines of up to 4% of global turnover.

Every government that debated this line is now watching one country run the experiment.

The case each side makes…

The Arguments

For the ban

A feed built on comparison lands hardest when identity is least formed.

A 12-year-old cannot consent to a trade they cannot understand.

Against

It removes a lifeline as well as a risk for isolated young people.

Enforcement means age checks for every user of every age.

Neither side disputes that the old limit of 13 failed. The fight is over what replaces it.
